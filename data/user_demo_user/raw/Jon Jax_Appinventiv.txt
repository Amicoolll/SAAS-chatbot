   2. Project Summary
The Proposed Platform will be a SAAS-based Multi-Tenant Electronic Health Record System. The system will be built for Recovery Residence Organizations(RRO), The Platform will have the ability for each RRO Tenant to allow Multiple Staff Logins, Portal (Client) logins, and User Access Control. The Platform will be HIPAA Compliant and will also have Tracking Capabilities for the Recovery Residence.
Onboarding: The Platform(SaaS) Administrators will be able to Give Users Access to Login over the Platform by either Providing the Login Credentials or the Required Access
Dashboard/Reporting: The Users will be able to view Numeral Stats for different Features over the Dashboard.
Manage Clinical Client Data: The Users will be able to View Different Information about the Patients over the Platform including General Info, Clinical information, Activity, Medical Information, etc.
Manage Organizational Data: The User will be able to View and Manage the Facility Data including the locations, beds, programs, Activities Calendar, etc.
Manage Organization and Client Financial Data: The Users will have the ability to View and Manage the Different Financial Data of the Organization as well as the Client over the Platform Itself
Data/File Export: The users will have the ability to Export and Transfer Documents and Files and Data outside the Platform
Document Generation and E-Signature: The Users will have the ability to Generate Documents over the platform regarding different Documentation Processes and then will also have the ability to Provide E-Signature for Verification and Validation.
Chat: The Platform will enable Different Stakeholders to chat within the Platform
Customizations:  The Users will be able to customize the Application Interface which will include Custom Themes, Access Controls, and Also Data Export
Lab Reports:  The Users will be able to View, Manage Lab Reports, Results over the Platform.

Google charges a one-time $25 fee to get a developer account on Google Play, which lets you publish Android apps. Free apps are distributed at no cost, and Google takes 30% of the revenues of paid apps for "carriers and billing settlement fees".  
Apple charges $99/year to join the iOS Developer Program which lets you publish apps for the iPhone, iPod Touch, and iPad. If you price your app for free there are no further charges. If you charge for your app, Apple takes 30%.
